PHOTOGRAPH YOUR VEHICLE FOR A QUICK SALE

A picture paints a thousand words. This hackneyed old saying has been around forever yet it remains true, especially when selling a used vehicle, be it car or commercial, motorcycle or motorhome. With the continuing rise of the internet as a sales tool buyers want to have a good hard look at an item before they start organising viewing trips and the like, and they will expect to see images. It falls to the seller to make sure that these photos are as good and as representative as they can possibly be.

THE FUTURE CHANGES FOR DRIVERS

There is a lack of consistency in the electric vehicle experience as far as consumers are concerned. The issue of range anxiety and, still, the lack of a large scale charging infrastructure continues to put potential buyers off, given the large number of super-economical petrol and diesel driven cars available for sale..

There is an inconsistency when it comes to showcasing electric cars in that right now the trend seems to be towards hybrid technology. In short, EV’s are taking a bit of a back seat and among car makers there does not seem to be a determined push to get more motorists behind the wheels of true electric vehicles. Variability in product knowledge and patchy EV inventory hinders sales, hence the continued promotion of non-electric vehicles.

DRIVE ECONOMY

Most of us, if pressed, will say that when we get a new car, the temptation is to just go out and drive for no reason. This is especially true if it is the car of our dreams. We've saved money at the expense of everything else, made money on selling our old vehicle by selling quickly online and done the deal of a lifetime. There's nothing wrong with using up a bit of fuel for pleasure: none at all. Is there?.

The trouble with the real world though is that it has a habit of reminding us about life. The fact is, in these costly times with economic worries ever present, it makes sense to conserve precious petrol when we can and the way to do that is by driving properly and taking care. This applies double if towing a caravan. To help, here's a few hints to do just that whilst still enjoying the motor and staying safe.

ACCIDENTS

We all love driving whether it is for business or pleasure and it is easy to forget that we are putting ourselves at risk on the busy roads. From time to time we drive past the scene of an accident and give thanks that it wasn't us. The trouble is, it is all too easy to become complacent and that's when it might just be our turn to be a road-side statistic. The fact is, accidents happen and there are always consequences.

Drivers who have been involved in a crash often report that they lose confidence behind the wheel. The thought of getting back in the driving seat can be daunting for some as fear takes over reason. It is not just the damage to the car or person, there is also the awful aftermath of paperwork and recrimination that takes its toll too..

BREXIT AND THE CAR INDUSTRY

Following on from last year’s Brexit vote, the UK government decided to hold another general election in the belief that they would gain a strong majority in Parliament in the run-up to Great Britain leaving the European Union.

The Brexit team keep going to Europe to maintain the discussions but from a weakened point of view and it is a worry for the business world and, given the size and scope of our home -grown motor industry, a major headache because nobody can foresee an outcome.
